Free Palestine


My Bibliography

As a kid, I've always dreamed of visiting Palestine (Occupied by Isreal) and seeing the holy mosques with my own eyes. I fantasized learning about the Palestinians past, their ancestors, and everything in between. However, like many others to this day, I was unaware of the women, men, and children living in the war zones of their country until later on in my life. Being so young, my parents had shielded me from the inhumane reality of Palestine. But, as I have grown up, I believe that the issues occurring cannot be covered up, and it is my job, as a free and equal Muslim teenager in America, to create awareness about the injustices being served to innocent people.I am of Egyptian Heritage, but as a Muslim in a democratic society, I grew up around Palestinians. My parents close friends were Palestinians, and their fight for freedom became ours too. Their people became our people, and their voice became our voice.

Where Did The Palestinians Go?

Now, you should be wondering where did the Palestinians go? Many fled to Jordan, leaving behind their jobs and loved ones. However, they were put into what some people call refugee camps. But, in reality, these camps had barbed wire fences, soldiers, curfews, and little food and supplies for the Palestinians to survive. While some called these camps “refugee camps,” they were really concentration camps in disguise. Today, the Israeli military and citizens are not given restrictions on how to treat the Palestinians. To make them suffer, the Israelis throw tear gas into people’s homes, blocking all escape exits. They even throw fake bombs into people’s homes as entertainment. The Israelis randomly raid Palestinian homes, taking money, food, electronics, and more. Is this how humans should be treated? Is this humane?

What can you do?

People say there is nothing we can do. They say one person will not make a difference, but that is not true. One person becomes ten, ten becomes one hundred, and one hundred becomes one thousand. In fact, we, as students, need to be knowledgeable about the issues around the world and take small actions here and there to minimize the support of injustices. For example, thousands of students have formed a club that holds events to spread awareness of the occupation called Students for Justice in Palestine. They have partnered with other civil rights organizations, like Black Lives Matter, to establish support for both causes. In addition, there are also numerous companies around the United States that support the Israeli military, and we must boycott their products. I am positive that we, Americans, do not want to support the Israeli government and be the cause of more deaths. However, the reality is: if you buy Israeli products, you are supporting the Jewish oppression upon the Palestinians.
